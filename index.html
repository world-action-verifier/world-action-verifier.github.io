Abstract

World models promise scalable policy evaluation, optimization, and planning, yet achieving the required level of robustness remains challenging. Unlike policy learning which primarily focuses on optimal actions, a general-purpose world model must be reliable over a much broader range of suboptimal actions, which are often insufficiently covered by action-labeled interaction data. To address this challenge, we present World Action Verifier (WAV), a framework that enables world models to identify their own prediction errors and self-improve. The key idea is to decompose action-conditioned state prediction into two factors—state plausibility and action reachability—and verify each separately. We show that these decomposed verification problems can be substantially easier than directly predicting future states due to two underlying asymmetries: the broader availability of action-free data and the lower dimensionality of action-relevant features. Leveraging these asymmetries, we augment a world model with (i) a diverse subgoal generator obtained from video corpora and (ii) a sparse inverse model that infers actions from a selected subset of state features. By enforcing cycle consistency among generated subgoals, inferred actions, and forward rollouts, WAV provides an effective verification mechanism in under-explored regimes, where existing methods typically fail. Across nine tasks spanning MiniGrid, RoboMimic, and ManiSkill, our method improves downstream policy performance by 18\% while reducing the action-labeled data budget by more than 50\%.

Analysis: Two Key Asymmetries

We formalize the "Verification Subset" $\mathcal{S}$ as a block of latent variables that is both insulated (independent of scene clutter) and action-influenced. The sparse inverse model $h_\psi$ operates solely on this subset:

$$\hat{\mathbf{a}}^{t} = h_\psi(\hat{\mathbf{z}}_{\mathcal{S}}^{t}, \hat{\mathbf{z}}_{\mathcal{S}}^{t+1})$$

Our theoretical analysis rests on the Generation-Verification Gap. Even when the full state-action pair $(\mathbf{z}^{t}, \mathbf{a}^{t})$ is out-of-support (OOS), the sub-block transition often satisfies two conditions:

In-Distribution Verifier: The subset pair remains on the training support:

$$(\mathbf{z}_{\mathcal{S}}^{t}, \mathbf{a}^{t}) \in \operatorname{supp}(P_{\text{seed}})$$

Identifiable Actions: The action leaves a unique, recoverable imprint on $\mathbf{z}_{\mathcal{S}}$.

Theorem (Informal): Under these conditions, the inverse verifier $h_\psi$ can correctly recover missing actions even on compositional OOS transitions, providing a robust signal for self-improvement.

Method: Asymmetric Forward-Inverse Consistency

Our objective is to learn a robust action-conditioned world model that satisfies two critical desiderata: high fidelity in state prediction and reliable controllability in under-explored regimes. Prior world models often collapse on the long tail of suboptimal actions due to the scarcity of labeled interaction data and the difficulty of verifying high-dimensional predictions. WAV remedies that by exploiting the asymmetry between generation and verification through a video-prior subgoal generator, a sparse inverse model, and a forward-inverse cycle. Leveraging the abundance of unlabeled videos for state plausibility and a sparsity constraint for action identifiability induces a rigorous self-verification mechanism.
